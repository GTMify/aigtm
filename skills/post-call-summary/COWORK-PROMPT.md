# Post-Call Summary — Cowork Prompt

```
I just got off a sales call. Turn my notes into three things:

1. **Internal summary:** Key discussion points, decisions made, objections raised, and any signals about budget, timeline, competition, or who's championing the deal
2. **Action items:** Every task that came out of the call — who owns it, when it's due, and priority level
3. **Follow-up email:** A professional email to the prospect that recaps what we discussed, confirms next steps, and ends with a clear next action. Match the tone of the call — if it was casual, keep the email casual.
4. **CRM notes:** A 2-3 sentence version I can paste into my CRM with next step and stage recommendation.

## Call context
Company: [Company Name]
Who was on the call: [Names and titles]
Call type: [Discovery / Demo / Negotiation / QBR / Check-in]
My name: [Your name]

## Rules
- Don't add anything that wasn't in my notes. If I didn't mention budget, put "Not discussed."
- Keep the prospect's exact words for objections — paraphrasing loses nuance.
- Keep the follow-up email under 200 words.
- If my notes are too thin to extract real action items, tell me what's missing.

## My call notes
[Paste your raw notes, transcript, or bullet points here — messy is fine]
```
